Virtus

Beyond the Gym Floor

My Role

Content Writer, UX Strategy, UI Design

Software

Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign

Timeframe

2 weeks

Anxiety, Insecurities, Intimidation - All common emotions felt by the average gym go-er, holding them back from achieving their potential.

Born from a personal passion, Virtus is a fictional fitness club designed to create an inclusive, non-intimidating environment.

Through the project, I aimed to create not just a space for physical activities, but a community where people feel comfortable to push beyond their insecurities. The content on the website was created to meet these needs by using motivating and encouraging language to create an inclusive environment. I made Virtus to be a place where members feel supported and welcomed.

Purpose, VP, Target Audiences

The purpose of the website is to showcase the wide range of amenities offered and differentiate ourselves from other fitness facilities. Our goal is to guide potential clients to sign-up for a membership by showcasing the benefits of Virtus. As an anti big-box gym, we stand behind authenticity and aim to create a welcoming environment that makes everyone feel supported no matter where they stand in their fitness journey. Throughout the website we want to inspire confidence and show that Virtus is more than just a gym - it’s a community focused on growth.

With an intended audience of all genders, ages 18-40, our primary audience consists of, fitness enthusiasts and athletes who are committed to their goals and willing to spend the extra dollar for the quality of the facility and amenities. In addition, the secondary audience includes fitness beginners, casual gym go-ers, and local residents of the Lower Mainland. Newer to fitness and chasing their health and wellness goals, these individuals may be looking for a community-oriented space to support their journey.

Research

To kickoff the project and ensure the success of Virtus, I started by conducting research involving analyzing trends in the fitness market, identifying the key competitors, and understanding the needs and preferences of our target audiences. A persona was developed to better understand each of their needs, motivations, and pain points. Additionally, an empathy map was created to help me capture their thoughts and feelings which helped me to tailor the website’s content to two types of users.

Identity

Virtus, inspired by the Latin virtue, came to mind when naming the company because it carries meanings of excellence, valor, courage, and strength – aligning perfectly with the company’s values. When designing the logo, I wanted to create something distinguishable. Initially, I experimented with simple letter marks of V’s and T’s while incorporating a subtle and unique feature to make it stand out. Though it may not be noticeable right away, I settled with a design that includes an old fashion spartan helmet into each side of the letter symbolizing resilience and discipline and perfectly ties the logo back to the origin of the name Virtus.

Written Content & Keyword Integration

At the beginning of the project, short and long-tailed keywords that potential customers would use on a search engine were listed and later incorporated into the written content of the webpage mock-up. As I developed the written content, I tried my best to make it as genuine as possible with some personality and tone to account for the social pressures people face when going to the gym. Even though the difference of this may be small, I believe it contributes to addressing for those concerns to create a more inclusive environment.

Wireframing & Prototyping

As per the requirements of the project, the wireframing was initially created in Adobe InDesign. I created a homepage, about us page, services, and an FAQ which were all essential pages for a fitness facility. These were a good reference for the final prototype made on Figma, where I incorporated components, used a design system and added a Memberships page.

Reflection

Designing the website mock-up for Virtus has been an amazing learning experience. While the project primarily focused on content writing for targeted audiences, it also highlighted the importance of addressing common design problems faced by Virtus' competitors. Being able to identify these pain points and implementing a solution Virtus could further differentiate itself from competitors and help the business stand out.

As I'm quite early on in my career, this project helped me understand the difficulties of tying web design, content creation, and user-centric approaches all together and although I'm proud of the work done for the project, there are numerous areas I'd hope to expand on.
